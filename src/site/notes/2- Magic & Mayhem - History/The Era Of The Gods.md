This Era is considered the most fictitious and has the least historical knowledge and proof to be fully documented decade by decade let alone year by year, what is know is mostly derived from the numerous religious texts from Roylar, [[Magic & Mayhem - Continents/Tholoth\|Tholoth]], Elgarian Seraphism and Nadiran texts, as you can expect we will divide this Era based on region rather than linear time, and we will start with Tholoth first.

## ***Tholoth's Truth***

*Tholoth's Truth is the oldest religious text and faith in the world of M&M, specifically the times when the Nelaeryns weren't the empire they are now and were just a minor clan who worshipped Tholoth. The text describes that Tholoth appeared from the Gates of Heclia[^1] and started the creation of the continent that is now named after him[^2], he eventually created the Elves derived from his likeness. In this creation myth, Tholoth isn't the only god, his sister Candia and his wife Derivia were the most mentioned and notable gods described, Candia was seen more as kind goddess who valued all life while Tholoth had a more harsh outlook that he shared with his wife Derivia who made the Dark elves in her image[^3]. The text goes on to talk about the months long process of creation and Tholoth's adventures which seem akin to what you would see either in a folklore or child book where every story teaches a lesson, there is a story about the creation of Greatwood[^4], there is another particular story, that mentions Tholoth's first encounter with a species he hadn't created[^6], where in it he turns to rage and sorrow over the fact such a creature could exist, untouched by his benevolence and deems them as curses to be vanquished.*

*Next, the Text goes to the more abrasive elements. In this section, Candia betrays her brother Tholoth after many instances of them disagreeing with each other. Candia fearing the damage a god war between siblings could do, takes her believers and creations and sails into the horizon[^5], there she makes her infamous Candianwood meant to give the other species of the world a chance against the elvish Greatwood. This betrayal doesn't go unnoticed by the other gods as they see it as a moment of weakness in Tholoth and plot to uproot him from his high throne, they are discovered by Derivia's creations and when the plot is revealed, a war against Tholoth by the lesser gods begun and ends with the battle of Heaven's Gate, where Tholoth injured from using his essence to give True Death to the lesser gods, is thus forced to go back to his domain with his fellow gods past the gates of Heclia where all believers of the faith eventually go and with that the era of walking gods ends in the continent.* 

## ***Roylar's Wisdom***

*The second oldest text of M&M, Roylar's wisdom was made during a gathering of all religions on the Continent of Roylar by its namesake, called The Gathering of Jicelia afterwards, it was an effort by Rex Roylar himself to unite the faiths of the continent into one so that it cannot be used as a tool of division by the enemies of his new empire. The text is primarily made up of creation myths and unifying myths inspired by the ancient tribes that roamed the continent before the arrival of the elves, from tales about Antelon to Carden to even Vendri the madman. There is less controversy here compared to Tholoth's Truth as this text focuses on stories for its believers rather than inter-god politics though there are still such minor cases. At the end, Roylar intentionally weaves the stories into one event where the gods unite against the Nelaeryns and proclaim him as their champion with some variations, that came after his death, making him a god as well, The Nadiran Faith also is mentioned here and it is said their sun kept watch and protected them from the elves and other calamities during this time though it doesn't depict Roylar as a hero of the gods or a god but a simple man who managed to catch an opportunity for freedom.*

*The Text eventually ends with the solemn note that the gods, weakened from blessing Roylar's slave legions, were forced to retreat to their domains and look over their children from afar.*

## ***Seraphism***

*Seraphism is the newest religion (respectively) on the block per say, it believes all gods are real, but only worships those that have 'favored' the Elgarian empire through them being major deities in the surviving faiths of the founder species of said empire, this text differs from the others as it has no stories of mortals ever actually interacting with the gods, the one slight exception is in the Lyran Manea mythology of 'descending gods', where their main deity descended from the heavens upon the world while lost and alone, and after being given kindness and assistance by the Manea people, she decided to adopt them as hers, which then with them being one of the founding species of Elgar and the entire event that caused the night to be important in Elgarian mythology and history, has made her the "head" of their pantheon after many of the founding species abandoned and distanced themselves from older "sun" and overall "sky father" deities due to the fact they see They see Tholoth as one such solar father figure as he is depicted as such in many Nelaeryn works*

*Seraphism as a whole has a focus on self betterment, as well as possessing ancestor worship, they have a less 'historical' basis on ancient events simply from the fact Elgar for all intents and purposes never pushed for a central faith with history to back it but instead for a faith by its peoples for its peoples.*

## ***The Eternal Flame***

*The Dawn Faith is the predominant religious tradition across the Nadiran Dunes, with its foundational text, "The Eternal Flame," serving as both theological doctrine and historical record. The text describes the creation of the world, the divine conflicts that shaped it, and the eventual ascension of the twin gods who now watch over humanity from the celestial realms. According to the faith, the world was born from divine struggle, and humanity itself was crafted from the remains of a primordial terror.*

*The text begins in the Age of Fire, when Shahrokh, the Sun God and Lord of Flames, ruled alone over a world of endless dunes and eternal light. Born from a pyre amidst the sands, Shahrokh grew by consuming the strength of fire itself, his power ascending as he wandered the primal world seeking challenges among the ancient monsters that predated all mortal life[^7]. From the chitin of these beasts he forged armor of midnight black veined with crimson and gold, and he clad himself in robes of shimmering leather. For eons he hunted, but grew dismayed when no creature could challenge him, his lust for battle left unsatisfied beneath the scorching rays of his own power.*

*The text then recounts the legendary encounter that would reshape the world. When Shahrokh ventured onto a plateau of salt, the ground shattered and a serpentine beast rose to the heavens with a roar that made the god's ears bleed[^8]. This primordial serpent, who called itself the First and the Last, had created the plains of salt and shaped the dunes in an age when only darkness existed. The battle between Shahrokh and the serpent raged for eons, their conflict giving birth to the very elements of the world and volcanoes rose from their underground struggles, winds and tornadoes emerged from their aerial combat, and rivers and valleys were carved by their colossal blows[^9]. The blood of both combatants fell to earth, mixing to create the first animals, while Shahrokh's light gave life to the first plants. The serpent's defeat marks the true beginning of mortal history. From its meat, Shahrokh molded humanity in his own image, breathing life into them with his blood and fire. From its bones he constructed the First City, a grand palace resting upon the beast's skull, and from its chitinous plates he built walls to shelter his new creation. From its hide he fashioned golden robes, that humanity might walk proudly as lords over all animals. Shahrokh ruled as the first Sultan, teaching his people to fight, conquer, and create new life[^10].*

*However, the Age of Fire contained within it the seeds of its own ending. The text describes how Shahrokh began to feel whispers when clouds passed over his city, and his flame flickered when he remained still for too long. Following the scent of the serpent's blood to a deep cavern, he encountered a figure draped in purple robes with a crescent crown, born from the eye and death of the ancient serpent. This was Yasamin, whose soothing voice sang a lullaby of sleep to the weary god. As Shahrokh succumbed to slumber in her embrace, she stepped into the world, and the first rain fell upon the sands becoming the first Caliph. The Age of Fire had ended, and the Age of Night had begun[^11]. Yasamin's reign was marked by abundant rains that transformed deserts into flourishing jungles. She transformed the souls of the departed into the first stars, scattering them across the night sky to guide humanity. She established the Astromancers as her chosen guardians, and humanity cast aside their golden robes for silver garments crafted from ores born from raindrops and sand. During this era, Shahrokh, weakened and tired, wandered among mortals while gathering his strength. He trained the first Fire Mages and infiltrated the palace in disguise, yet through living among humans, he came to understand the flaws of his previous rule and grew to respect Yasamin. Their friendship deepened into love, transcending the differences that had once driven them apart. The text describes how conflict nonetheless erupted when one of Yasamin's Astromancers exposed Shahrokh's identity, forcing the god to flee and his followers to rise in rebellion. But amidst the devastation, Shahrokh and Yasamin chose reconciliation over destruction, setting aside their grievances to save humanity from the chaos they had unleashed. Their union marked the end of the Age of Night, and the beginning of the undying Age of Dawn.*

*In the final passages, the text describes the divine couple's ascension. Recognizing that their direct rule had become untenable as humanity multiplied, they chose the first Sultans to guide the people and the first Caliph to guide the Sultans, with the Astromancers and the Order of Fire serving as councilors bridging the mortal and divine realms. Shahrokh and Yasamin ascended with the First City to the celestial realms, where it became an eternal beacon of light. The lush jungles gave way to the familiar dunes and oases, and the cycle of day and night began. The Dawn Faith, laid down by the gods before their ascension, emphasizes unity, respect, the pursuit of knowledge, and the sacred balance between fire and water, light and shadow, that sustains all creation[^12].*

[^7]: *The "ancient monsters" are understood by scholars to represent the primordial chaos that existed before divine order was established. The text's description of Shahrokh creating armor from their chitin has been interpreted as the imposition of divine will upon formless creation. The specific colors described as midnight black with red and orange veins, and robes of pure gold have become the sacred colors of the Order of Fire.*

[^8]: *The serpent's identity has been subject to extensive theological debate. Most mainstream interpretations hold that the serpent represented the darkness and chaos that predated divine order, though heterodox traditions occasionally suggest the serpent was a primordial god in its own right. The description of the serpent's face unfolding into three petals with three spines around a central eye has been seen as a metaphor for the tripartite nature of existence, that being, creation, preservation, and dissolution.*

[^9]: *The battle is understood to have been both literal and symbolic. The text's description of the gods' blood mixing to create animals, and their conflict giving birth to geological features, established the foundational principle that divine struggle underlies the natural order.*

[^10]: *The First City's identity remains debated among scholars of the Dawn Faith. Some traditions hold that it ascended with the gods and is now a celestial realm, while others believe its ruins lie somewhere beneath the sands, waiting to be rediscovered. The city's architecture which is built from the serpent's bones and chitin, is described in later texts as possessing a luminescent quality, suggesting the divine essence still lingered in its construction.*

[^11]: *Yasamin's creation from the serpent's eye and her role as goddess of night, stars, and rain has been interpreted as representing the necessary complementary aspect of divine power. Her palace in the moon and her transformation of souls into stars established the doctrine that mortal life continues in a transformed state beyond death, providing the foundation for the faith's eschatology.*

[^12]: *The reconciliation between Shahrokh and Yasamin forms the ethical core of the Dawn Faith. Their decision to set aside divine conflict for the sake of mortal welfare established the principle that love and wisdom must guide power. The establishment of the Sultans as mortal rulers and the Caliph as their better, with the Astromancers and Order of Fire as advisors, created the political and religious structure that endures in the Caliphate to this day. The "undying dawn" of the Age of Dawn refers not to a static state but to the perpetual renewal of day and night, representing the ongoing balance of divine forces.*
